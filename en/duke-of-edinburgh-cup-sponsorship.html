The Official Partner of The Duke of Edinburgh Cup

ATFX is proud to announce the official partnership of The Duke of Edinburgh Cup, UK Qualifying Event 2025 and we are excited to continue to support this established charity. Our company is passionate about social responsibility and we are dedicated to supporting causes such as this one.

WHY ATFX BECAME PATRON OF THE DUKE OF EDINBURGH CUP

Originated in the UK

The Duke of Edinburgh Cup is a sports tournament that originated in the UK and has expanded into various regions around the world. ATFX is an FCA regulated company based in the UK and the company is also expanding into other regions, making the partnership with DOE highly compatible.

Global Charity Action

ATFX has branched out to communities globally in order to actively care and support societies and people in need. We wanted to work with the DOE cup for its dedication to help young people worldwide. We think it is highly important to look after and nurture the next generation, so this is very much in line with ATFX’s philosophy.

Branded Sporting Events

The Duke of Edinburgh Cup is based on a 25-year brand development.

About The Duke of Edinburgh Cup

The Duke of Edinburgh Cup, a charity with Royal Patronage dedicated to helping young people world-wide by providing and giving support in health, education and welfare. Established in 2000, events have since been held in over 24 countries with £5.3 million(over USD 6,734,000) raised for charities and good causes that have helped children and young people overcome hardship and adversity to lead better lives. For those who support us in our events, whether as a participant or a sponsor, it is about enjoying a sport we enjoy whilst making a difference to those less fortunate than us.

How it works

The Duke of Edinburgh Cup is an annual international golf series organized by International Golf for Youth. Each event held in a country or region during the year becomes that year’s national ‘The Duke of Edinburgh Cup.’ These competitions follow a handicap format in the Stableford style, adhering to R & A or USGA rules.

At least two Qualifiers from each event are invited to participate in the World Finals of The Duke of Edinburgh Cup, held in England during the autumn. The expenses for these Qualifiers, which include air travel, hotel accommodation, transportation, a welcome dinner, three rounds of golf, and the Prize Giving Dinner at Windsor Castle, are covered. This series of events is sanctioned by the R & A for expense payments, but no direct expenses are paid to the qualifiers.

If an individual event raises sufficient funds, the trustees of IGFY may, at their discretion, increase the number of qualifiers from that event.

The 36-hole World Final spans two days and takes place at two prestigious golf courses near London. Additionally, there is a friendly match with members of the Royal Household Golf Club, located at Windsor Castle.

The culmination of the event is a formal black-tie prize-giving dinner held in the State Rooms of Windsor Castle, graced by the presence of a member of the Royal Family.
